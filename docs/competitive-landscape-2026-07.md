# Competitive landscape — 2026-07

Internal positioning doc, not for publication. Written from the TODOS.md
item ("competitive one-pager") queued by the 2026-07-02 roadmap review.
Scope: three quadrants that could plausibly overlap with Obscurity Engine —
taste-toys, artist analytics, and open listening infra. Findings are from
July 2026 web research; cited inline.

## Quadrant table

| Who | What they do | What they DON'T do that we do |
|---|---|---|
| **Obscurify** | Spotify-connected "obscurity rating" (percentile vs. other users' taste) + genre/mood breakdown + an "Obscure Picks" playlist of suggested artists/songs.[^obscurify] | Scoring is the whole product — reach/discovery is a side playlist, not a ranked, re-runnable, cross-validated recommendation pipeline. No underexplored-vs-new distinction, no obscurity ceiling definition, no artist-side surface. |
| **stats.fm** | Deep personal listening analytics (10M+ users, spans 100M tracks); users can already filter their own top artists by popularity to spot the obscure ones in their own history.[^statsfm] | "Obscurity and Fan Ranking" is an **open feature request**, not shipped — stats.fm surfaces obscurity *within your existing history*, it does not recommend artists you haven't heard yet.[^statsfm-feedback] No recommendation engine at all. |
| **Receiptify** | Generates a shareable "receipt" graphic of your top tracks/artists. Pure visualization, no scoring, no recommendation.[^receiptify] | Everything — it's a share-card generator, not a discovery tool. Useful comparison only as the proof that a taste-toy share artifact can go viral (relevant to our own `/r/` and `/u/` pages, not a competitor on substance). |
| **Spotify for Artists** | Free analytics for any artist with a Spotify catalog: streams, saves, listener demographics, a "listener journey" funnel (new → casual → dedicated), Discovery Mode (pay-for-boost via royalty trade-off), 2026 editorial-support programs for emerging artists.[^s4a][^s4a-2026] | No cross-platform obscurity framing, no "distance to N true fans" metric, no way for a *listener* to discover them via an obscurity lens — it's an artist-facing dashboard, not a discovery surface, and it's Spotify-catalog-only. |
| **Chartmetric** | Multi-platform (Spotify/Apple/TikTok/Instagram/YouTube) analytics, playlist-pitching fit scores, brand-partnership matching. Artist plan from ~$5–8/mo, Premium ~$116/mo, built primarily for labels/managers.[^chartmetric] | No listener-facing discovery product at all — it's a B2B tool for the artist's team, not something a fan ever touches. Its own review data shows sub-100K-listener artists get more value from raw behavioral metrics (save rate, listener/follower ratio) than from its A&R-oriented discovery tooling — i.e. even Chartmetric's own users say it under-serves the obscure end.[^chartmetric-2] |
| **Soundcharts** | Real-time playlist/chart tracking, radio monitoring (74 countries), social analytics, benchmarking against 5M artists. $10–129/mo tiers.[^soundcharts] | Same shape as Chartmetric: momentum/chart tracking for teams, not a discovery engine for listeners, and no "1000 true fans" or reach-relative-to-engagement framing found anywhere in its 2026 feature set. |
| **ListenBrainz / MusicBrainz ecosystem** | Open, non-profit, collaborative-filtering recommendations (`troi-recommendation-playground`) that power ListenBrainz's own weekly-jam/weekly-exploration playlists and third-party tools like Explo (self-hosted Discover-Weekly-alternative) and academic CF research.[^lb][^lb-troi][^lb-explo] | No obscurity ceiling, no "underexplored vs. new" novelty model, no artist mission framing, no product surface aimed at listeners browsing for undiscovered artists — it's recommendation *infrastructure*, and every consumer built on it (including us, via the blend candidate source) has to add the obscurity-first framing themselves. |
| **Last.fm (own recs)** | "Recommended Radio" weekly mixes + a 2026 "Hide Artist" suppression feature (cosine similarity on cached tag vectors) to reduce repetitive suggestions.[^lastfm] | No obscurity concept whatsoever — Last.fm's own recommender is popularity-agnostic-by-default (arguably popularity-*positive*, since `artist.getsimilar` is the exact popularity-biased graph our data-ceiling finding is built on). We are, structurally, a filter Last.fm itself doesn't apply to its own graph. |

## Why each is structurally unlikely to build obscurity-first discovery

- **Taste-toys (Obscurify/stats.fm/Receiptify):** their business model is virality off a *personal* stat (a percentile, a receipt), not a recommendation outcome. Obscurity is content for a share card, not a product to optimize adoption against — there's no reason for them to build attributed-adoption tracking or an artist-side surface, because their loop closes at the share, not at a discovery.
- **Artist analytics (Spotify for Artists/Chartmetric/Soundcharts):** their customer is the artist's team, and their revenue model (subscriptions, brand-matching commissions, or Spotify's own Discovery Mode royalty trade) depends on metrics the team already has budget to chase (playlist placements, chart momentum, brand deals) — not on manufacturing net-new *listener* discovery of obscure artists, which has no direct monetization path for them and would cannibalize the premium tiers built around "help you get bigger" framing.
- **Open infra (ListenBrainz/Last.fm):** both are non-profit or hobbyist-adjacent infrastructure projects; their mandate is listening-history preservation and general-purpose recommendation, not a specific obscurity mission. Building obscurity-first discovery on top of them is exactly what a downstream product (us) is for — it is not in scope for the infra maintainers themselves, and nothing here suggests it's becoming so.
- **Spotify itself (API policy):** the Feb 2026 Development Mode changes (5-user cap, Premium-account requirement, one Client ID per developer) and the 2025-04-15 Extended Access tightening (250K+ MAU, registered organization) *raise the bar for anyone building recommendation apps on Spotify data* going forward, us included — but existing large apps are grandfathered (existing Dev-Mode apps keep access above 5 users; Extended-access apps are unaffected).[^feb2026][^ext-access] stats.fm (10M+ users) and Receiptify are almost certainly well past whatever tier they need already; Obscurify's exact standing is unconfirmed but it long predates the 2025-2026 tightening. None of this pressures the incumbents to pivot toward obscurity-first discovery — if anything it raises the cost of anyone *new* trying to compete with them on a Spotify-API-native taste-toy, while leaving us mostly unaffected because our core value doesn't sit on the Spotify API (see below).

## Our durable edges, as of today

1. **Validated reach improvement via the Last.fm+ListenBrainz blend.** The n=348 confirmatory eval shows the blend candidate source moves pooled reach +0.0066 absolute (+9.5% relative, 95% CI [+0.0039, +0.0100], excludes zero, significant in all three anchors) with no regression on obscW@k or pure-discovery hits.[^blend] This is a first-party, pre-registered, statistically validated result — none of the quadrants above publish anything comparable for an obscurity-specific metric, because none of them measure obscurity-specific reach at all.
2. **We are not dependent on the Spotify API for core value.** Seeds, candidates, and scoring run entirely on Last.fm + (as of the blend) ListenBrainz/MusicBrainz — public APIs with no 250K-MAU eligibility wall. Every incumbent above except the open-infra quadrant is Spotify-API-native and now operates under a tightening policy regime; we are structurally insulated from it by architecture, not luck.
3. **We are starting to accumulate exactly the dataset the eval proved is missing:** first-party attributed-adoption events (Phase 1, F2/F3) and a listener time-series built from getinfo calls we already make and previously discarded (F4). No competitor above has an attributed "did we cause this discovery" metric — stats.fm and Obscurify measure taste, Chartmetric/Soundcharts measure chart/playlist momentum, ListenBrainz measures nothing product-specific. This is the same asset class as the blend result: something that has to be *earned* over time, not bought.
4. **Artist-side mission framing has no analog.** "Distance to your true 1000 fans," reframed as engagement-relative under-reach, doesn't exist in any tool surveyed — Spotify for Artists gets closest with its listener-journey funnel, but it's an artist-facing analytics dashboard with a "grow bigger" frame, not a discovery-causation frame tied to an obscurity ceiling.

## Our real weaknesses vs. them

- **Tiny user base.** No confirmed MAU figure exists yet (analytics baseline is still pending per the roadmap's E1), but it is certainly orders of magnitude below stats.fm's 10M+ or Receiptify's Wrapped-adjacent virality peaks.
- **Last.fm-scrobbler niche.** Every incumbent above except the open-infra quadrant is Spotify-native, i.e. built for where the listeners already are. We require a Last.fm account with scrobble history to unlock the account-linked flow at all (Spotify linking is Phase 2, capped by the same 5-user Dev Mode ceiling documented in `spike-spotify-quota-2026-07-03.md`).
- **No mobile app.** All the taste-toy competitors are optimized for a mobile-first, Wrapped-adjacent share moment; we're a desktop-skewing web tool today.
- **5-user Spotify cap.** Even once F5 ships, Spotify-account-linked discovery is a manually-curated beta capped at 5 authenticated users until we clear a 250K-MAU/registered-business bar we are nowhere near — a constraint none of the Spotify-native incumbents above currently face (they cleared it, or were grandfathered, years before the bar moved).

## Positioning implications for the roadmap

1. **Don't compete on the share-card/virality axis directly** — Obscurify/Receiptify have the network effects and mobile polish there; our `/r/` and `/u/` pages (F7) should borrow the *mechanic* (shareable, screenshot-friendly) but win on substance (a validated recommendation, not just a stat), not try to out-viral them.
2. **The Spotify-API independence is a moat to protect, not a gap to close aggressively.** F5/F6 (Spotify linking) should stay scoped as the small opt-in beta the spike already recommends — over-investing engineering time chasing Spotify's shrinking developer surface would trade away the one structural advantage (no 250K-MAU wall) we currently have over every Spotify-native competitor.
3. **The artist-side mission surface (F10, "path to 1000 true fans") is genuinely uncontested** — nobody in the artist-analytics quadrant frames growth as engagement-relative under-reach, and none of the taste-toys touch the artist side at all. This is the single item on the roadmap with no competitive analog anywhere in this landscape; it should stay prioritized as the long-run differentiator once Phase 1's attributed-adoption data exists to seed it.
